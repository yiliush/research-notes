# Pythagora

Pythagora is the company behind GPT Pilot — one of the earliest viral open-source AI software engineers, with ~33,700 GitHub stars on `Pythagora-io/gpt-pilot`. The company has since broadened from pure migration/refactor to an "all-in-one AI development platform," though much of its momentum came from the original developer-facing OSS project.

## Origin

Founded in 2023 by Zvonimir Sabljic and Sasha Bilic. The team built GPT Pilot as one of the first projects that tried to make GPT-4 write entire applications end-to-end with human-in-the-loop checkpoints. Joined Y Combinator W24.

## Funding & Traction

- Total raised: ~$4M (seed).
- GitHub stars: ~33,700 on `Pythagora-io/gpt-pilot` — one of the highest in the AI coding agent space.
- Revenue: $900K ARR reported at time of YC interview (mostly bootstrap-era).

## Product

Two layers:

1. **GPT Pilot (OSS).** Open-source AI developer that writes full apps step by step, with the developer reviewing each step. Originated the "interactive code-by-the-step" pattern many later agents copied.
2. **Pythagora Pro.** Commercial managed service — Pythagora App Lab — that runs GPT Pilot with optimized models and prepaid credits.

The pivot has been from "AI builds your app" toward "AI maintains and migrates your app" and now back toward generic app building, suggesting positioning has not fully settled.

## Pricing

- GPT Pilot OSS: free, BYOK.
- Pythagora Pro: monthly subscription with prepaid credits (specific tiers not consistently published).

## Differentiators

- **GitHub star momentum.** 33.7k stars is the cheapest distribution moat in the cluster — every search for "AI developer" surfaces them.
- **Step-by-step paradigm.** Originated the interactive-checkpoint UX that became standard.
- **Y Combinator W24 alumni** with credible technical brand.

## Risk factors

- Horizontal coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Devin) have eaten the "AI builds your app" use case.
- Positioning has shifted multiple times — migration → app builder → all-in-one — which dilutes brand.
- Star count is impressive but conversion to paid users at this funding stage is thin.
- Not a true specialty agent anymore; closer to a horizontal app builder that started in the specialty category.
