# AI-Native IDEs

This cluster covers full editors built natively around AI agents — distinct from extensions to existing IDEs (Copilot, Continue), terminal-based agents (Claude Code, Codex CLI), and full autonomous platforms (Devin standalone, Replit Agent standalone). The category emerged in late 2023 with Cursor's launch and the Codeium/Windsurf rebrand, and by mid-2026 it has bifurcated into three sub-archetypes: (1) the VS Code-fork hyperscalers (Cursor, Windsurf, Trae) competing on agent quality, proprietary models, and indexing; (2) the ground-up structural alternative (Zed) competing on performance and an open-protocol bet; and (3) the browser-native runtime-first builder (Replit) competing on end-to-end "ship a deployed app" rather than "make engineers faster." The market is concentrated — Cursor alone is at $2B ARR and ~$50B valuation talks — but the Windsurf saga (Google's reverse-acquihire of CEO Varun Mohan for $2.4B, Cognition's 72-hour acquisition of the remainder) showed that even category leaders are subject to violent M&A reordering when the underlying models and talent are this scarce.

## Entities
- [[Cursor]] — Anysphere — category leader by revenue and valuation
- [[Windsurf]] — now owned by Cognition; survivor of the July 2025 Google/Cognition split
- [[Zed]] — Rust-based, open source, performance-focused outsider
- [[Trae]] — ByteDance's AI IDE; cheap, global, geopolitically charged
- [[Replit]] — browser-native cloud IDE; vibe-coding-adjacent; runtime+hosting moat

## Cross-Cutting Themes
- Proprietary coding models (Cursor's Composer, Cognition's SWE-1.5) replacing pure frontier-model reliance
- Codebase indexing and retrieval as a primary axis of differentiation
- Agent harnesses converging on multi-step plan/execute/observe loops with tool use and parallelism
- MCP and emerging protocols (Zed's ACP) standardizing how agents integrate with editors
- Enterprise revenue concentration (Cursor at 60%, Windsurf doubling QoQ) signaling category maturity
