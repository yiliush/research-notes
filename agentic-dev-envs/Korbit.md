# Korbit

Korbit (korbit.ai) is a Montreal-based code review startup with a tutorial-style angle: instead of just flagging issues, the agent explains why something is a problem and educates junior developers. It's free for open-source and aggressively priced for paid plans.

## Origin

Founded in 2020 by Iulian Serban and Jingcheng Niu, both with NLP research backgrounds (Serban came from Mila / Yoshua Bengio's lab). Korbit originally pitched itself as an AI mentor for developers learning to code before pivoting to the team/enterprise PR review market.

## Funding & Traction

- Seed-stage company; specific round sizes not widely disclosed (Crunchbase shows modest pre-Series A funding).
- Korbit Pro is free for all open-source repositories — a deliberate distribution play.

## Product

- Automated PR code review with explanatory comments.
- AI chat threaded on review comments — junior devs can ask "why" and get pedagogical answers.
- Auto-generated PR descriptions.
- Custom team rules.
- GitHub, GitLab, Bitbucket support.

## Pricing

- Starter: free, with limited PR reviews (~5/month).
- Pro: $9/user/month annual, $12/user monthly (some sources cite $24 — pricing has fluctuated).
- Free for all OSS repositories.
- Enterprise: custom.

## Differentiators

- **Pedagogical framing.** Tone of comments is explanatory, designed for developer education. Useful at orgs with many junior engineers.
- **OSS-free posture.** Helps with bottom-up adoption.
- **Canadian / academic founder lineage.** NLP research credibility, which they market to enterprise.

## Risk factors

- Pricing has been inconsistent across the market (different sources list different rates), which signals positioning uncertainty.
- Significantly less funded than [[CodeRabbit]] / [[Greptile]] — needs to find a defensible niche fast.
- "Educational" code review is a feature, not a category. Competitors can copy the tone overnight.
