# Ellipsis

Ellipsis (ellipsis.dev) is a Y Combinator W24 PR review startup with one notable differentiator: it doesn't just review — it can auto-fix and push commits back to the PR. The pricing is simple ($20/dev/month, unlimited) and the company stayed lean post-YC rather than chasing a large Series A.

## Origin

Founded in 2024 by Nicholas Moy and James Murdza, YC W24 batch. Both founders are technical with backgrounds in ML tooling.

## Funding & Traction

- $2M seed via YC, no announced follow-on round.
- Free for public GitHub repos (OSS distribution).
- Small team, stayed lean.

## Product

- Automated PR review with line comments.
- **AI Fixes**: Ellipsis can generate and push commits to fix issues it identifies — not just suggest.
- Custom "Style Guides" — natural-language rules the agent enforces.
- Slack integration.
- GitHub-native.

## Pricing

- Free for public GitHub repos, no feature limits.
- Business: $20/developer/month for unlimited use on private repos.
- Enterprise: custom with SSO, SLA, priority support.
- 7-day trial.
- Moved from per-commit to seat-based pricing in 2025.

## Differentiators

- **Auto-fix commits.** Most reviewers leave suggestions; Ellipsis ships code. Closer to a true agent than a commenter.
- **Style guide system.** Natural-language coding rules give it a customization edge.
- **Pricing simplicity.** $20 unlimited is the cleanest price in the category.

## Risk factors

- Smallest funding among credible competitors. Risks being out-marketed by [[CodeRabbit]] and outraised by [[Greptile]].
- Auto-commit feature carries trust risk — one bad fix erodes adoption fast.
- No standout customer logos disclosed.
