# v0

v0 is Vercel's generative UI and full-stack app builder. It started as a
shadcn/ui component generator in October 2023 and grew into Vercel's
strategic answer to Bolt and Lovable. Type a prompt, get a Next.js app
deployed to Vercel.

## Origin

v0 launched in October 2023 as a prompt-to-React-component tool, built by
Vercel as a way to make shadcn/ui (the design system Vercel underwrites)
the default visual vocabulary for AI-generated UI. Through 2024 it grew
into a chat-based interface and added backend generation. By late 2025 it
was Vercel's flagship growth product and a stand-alone subscription line.

## Funding & Traction

v0 is a Vercel product, not a standalone company. Vercel raised a $250M
Series E at a $3.25B valuation in May 2024 and has not disclosed standalone
v0 ARR. Internally, Vercel has positioned v0 as a material driver of
platform pull-through (every v0 app deploys to Vercel by default).

A major platform update on February 4, 2026 added Git integration, a
full VS Code-style editor, and improved preview surfaces — closing the
"but where is my code" complaint that had pushed serious users toward
Bolt.

## Product

You start in chat. The model proposes UI in a preview pane (often
multiple variants). You accept, iterate, or fork. Code is visible and
editable. The output is real Next.js code using shadcn/ui, Tailwind, and
the Vercel AI SDK conventions.

**Generated stack (opinionated)**: Next.js (App Router), shadcn/ui,
Tailwind, TypeScript. Backend integrations through Vercel — Neon Postgres,
Upstash, Vercel Blob, Vercel AI SDK. Auth via Auth.js or Clerk.

**Deployment**: One-click to Vercel. No first-class story for other hosts.

**Models**: Vercel ships a v0-tuned model (`vercel/v0-1.0-md` and
successors) as the default; users on higher tiers can route to other
frontier models.

## Differentiators

- **shadcn/ui as house style.** v0 output looks like every other shadcn
  app, which is either a feature (consistent, accessible, production-
  quality default) or a tell (you can spot v0-generated apps at a
  glance). The aesthetic homogenisation of the modern web traces partly
  through v0.
- **Vercel pull-through.** Every app deploys to Vercel. v0 is partly a
  customer acquisition channel for the hosting business and is priced
  accordingly — credits are cheap relative to Lovable.
- **Distribution.** Vercel has the largest installed base of Next.js
  developers on the planet. v0 inherits that.
- **Hybrid positioning.** v0 sits between non-engineer tools (Lovable)
  and pro-dev tools (Cursor). It wants both audiences. The February 2026
  IDE update is the pro-dev concession.
