# Second.dev

Second.dev is a small Y Combinator-backed company that pivoted from a general-purpose AI dev tool (W23 batch) to a focused codebase migration platform. The product is narrow: automate framework upgrades and large-scale refactors on a per-PR basis, billed by lines of code changed.

## Origin

YC W23 batch. Original product was a broader AI development tool that didn't find traction. Team relaunched in 2024 with a sharper focus on enterprise codebase maintenance and migrations.

## Funding & Traction

- Pre-seed: $1.9M.
- Small team, lean operations.
- Limited public customer disclosure.

## Product

- AI agents that perform codebase migrations and upgrades.
- GitHub integration; reviews and produces PRs.
- Codebase intelligence layer to understand the repo before migrating.

## Pricing

- Starter: free, up to 10,000 lines of code changes, 2 users, GitHub integration.
- Pro: $200/month for unlimited LOC changes.

The line-of-code pricing is unusual in this category — most competitors charge per seat or per project.

## Differentiators

- **Per-LOC pricing.** Aligns cost with actual migration scope.
- **YC network.** Strong distribution into other YC companies for upgrades.
- **Lean execution.** Operates at small team size, possibly profitable on $200/mo per customer.

## Risk factors

- Significantly less funded than [[Moderne]] or even [[Grit.io]] (pre-acquisition).
- Migration is a one-shot purchase — repeat business depends on continuous framework churn.
- LOC pricing scales linearly with cost (more tokens = more LLM spend) but linearly with revenue. Margin unclear.
