# Softgen

Softgen is the budget contender in the vibe coding category. A
$33-per-year membership plus wallet-based AI token top-ups makes it the
cheapest AI app builder by a wide margin. Acquired by Arising Ventures.

## Origin

Built as a no-frills AI app builder for the indie-hacker tier of the
market. Acquired by Arising Ventures (no public deal terms). The pricing
model — pay almost nothing up front, top up tokens as you use them — is
deliberately positioned against the $20-30/mo SaaS subscription default.

## Funding & Traction

- Acquired by Arising Ventures
- Reported ~$550K yearly revenue
- ~175,000 users
- No additional public funding rounds

This places Softgen as a profitable small-scale operator rather than a
venture-scale rocket. In a category dominated by $100M-$400M ARR
incumbents, that is a different posture, not a worse one.

## Product

Generates full-stack web applications with databases, auth, and APIs.
The output is a real working product, not a mockup. The platform handles
hosting.

**Generated stack**: Modern web stack with database and auth pre-wired.
Less opinionated than v0 (which is Next.js + shadcn) or Lovable (which
is React + Supabase).

**Deployment**: Hosted on the Softgen platform.

## Pricing (May 2026)

- Membership: $33/year (the platform access fee)
- AI usage: pay-as-you-go, wallet-based — top up funds, spend on
  generation only when active

This is the only major platform in the category with a true
pay-as-you-go bottom tier and no monthly subscription floor. For sporadic
users (a builder who ships one app every few months) Softgen is
mathematically the cheapest option.

## Differentiators

- **Cheapest in category by an order of magnitude.** $33/year vs.
  $25-30/month from competitors.
- **Wallet-based metering** removes the "did I cancel that subscription"
  burden that suppresses sporadic use of $25/mo tools.
- **Profitable small scale.** Not chasing $1B ARR. The product can
  survive without venture growth pressure.
- **Acquired by a holding company**, which signals stability over
  hypergrowth.

The trade-off: Softgen does not match the polish, model access, or
support of Bolt/Lovable/v0. The cheap tier exists because the user gets
less hand-holding.
