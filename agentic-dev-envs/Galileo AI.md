# Galileo AI

Galileo AI was the canonical text-to-UI startup of the 2023–2024 generation — type a prompt, get an editable hi-fi wireframe — and the first design-to-code company to be absorbed by a hyperscaler. Google acqui-hired Galileo in May 2025 and folded the team and the engine into Google Labs as Stitch. The standalone product no longer exists; the entry persists here because Galileo's design choices (text-to-multi-screen flows, Figma export, framework-agnostic output) set the template the rest of the design-to-code cluster now imitates.

## Origin
- Founded 2022 by Arnav Mathur and Arpit Mathur.
- Initial product: text-prompt → single editable UI screen, exportable to Figma or front-end code.
- Acquired by Google in May 2025 (acqui-hire; terms not disclosed). Team joined Google, product rebranded as Stitch under Google Labs.

## Adoption
- Pre-acquisition: significant community traction on Twitter/X among indie designers and PMs; cited by Karpathy and others as early evidence of "vibe design."
- Post-acquisition: usage migrated to Stitch, which is free during Google Labs preview.

## Product (Pre-Acquisition)
- Text-to-UI — describe a screen, Galileo generated a hi-fi wireframe with realistic copy and components.
- Figma export — flat to editable Figma frames.
- Code export — early multi-framework HTML/CSS output.
- Image-to-UI — upload a sketch, get a polished version.

## Pricing (Pre-Acquisition)
- Tiered subscription (free trial, paid plans ~$19/month for individuals); not relevant post-acquisition.

## Differentiators
- First to ship text-to-multi-screen at acceptable quality — preceded Stitch's 2.0 multi-screen feature by more than a year.
- Acquired before it had to figure out monetization at scale — which is why Stitch can run free in Google Labs without revenue pressure.
- Google's bet via Galileo: the design-to-code surface is a wedge into the front-end developer workflow, complementary to Gemini's coding push.
