# Create.xyz

Create.xyz (now rebranded "Anything") is a prompt-to-app builder
targeting non-engineer makers. Apps come with bundled backend
infrastructure — auth, database, Stripe payments — so a user with no
backend literacy still ships something that can take money.

## Origin

Launched as Create.xyz with a simple pitch: type what you want, get a
working app, deploy with one click. Rebranded to "Anything" in late
2025/early 2026 to signal a broader scope (mobile and web from one
prompt).

## Funding & Traction

- Crunchbase profile exists under "Anything" / "Create"; specific round
  amounts and recent ARR not publicly disclosed at granularity comparable
  to Lovable or Bolt
- Smaller scale than the top three, but distinct positioning on
  no-engineer ICP

## Product

Single chat input. The platform turns the prompt into a fully functional
web or mobile app with backend infrastructure baked in: auth, database,
Stripe payments. Credits meter the AI usage; the free tier is generous
enough to evaluate.

**Generated stack**: React frontend, managed backend with auth, database,
and Stripe payments integrated by default. Mobile output through React
Native paths.

**Deployment**: Hosted on the platform with custom domain options on
paid tiers.

## Pricing (May 2026)

Credit-based subscription with Free, Pro, and Max tiers. Credits are
consumed per generation. Pricing details shift; the headline framing is:

- Free: try the platform with capped daily credits
- Pro: heavy usage, custom domains, larger credit allotment
- Max: production workflow allowance

## Differentiators

- **Payments out of the box.** Stripe is wired up by default. For an
  indie hacker who wants to charge $9.99 for an app idea, this removes
  the largest non-product friction.
- **Mobile and web from one prompt.** The "Anything" rebrand reflects
  the cross-platform output ambition, narrower in Bolt/Lovable.
- **Non-engineer first.** Code visibility is deliberately downplayed.
  Closer to Lovable than Bolt in philosophy.
- **Smaller team and smaller hype** than the category leaders, which
  may matter for users who prefer not to be a rounding-error customer
  inside a hyperscaling company.
