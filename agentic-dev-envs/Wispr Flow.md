# Wispr Flow

Wispr Flow is the dictation app that broke out of the developer niche in 2025 and made voice-as-input-method a mainstream productivity story. By May 2026 it was reportedly closing a ~$260M round at a ~$2B valuation, nearly tripling its prior $700M mark and signing 125 enterprise customers per week — a trajectory that puts it in the same conversation as Wispr's hardware ambitions and Cursor's early enterprise blitz.

## Origin
- Founded by Tanay Kothari (CEO). San Francisco.
- Originally a hardware company (Wispr) targeting a neural wristband for silent input; pivoted Flow into the breakout product.
- Funding (cumulative ~$81M before the 2026 round):
  - 2024 seed funding from Spark Capital and others.
  - 2025 Series A: $30M led by Menlo Ventures, with NEA, 8VC, Evan Sharp, Henry Ward participating.
  - Nov 2025 Series A extension: $25M led by Notable Capital (Flight Fund participating).
  - May 2026: reportedly in talks for ~$260M at ~$2B valuation, led by Menlo Ventures.

## Adoption
- 2.5 million downloads between late 2025 and early 2026.
- 270 of the Fortune 500 reported as customers, including Nvidia and Amazon.
- Reported pace of ~125 new enterprise customers per week.
- Monthly user growth >50%; six-month retention ~80%; payment rate ~19%.
- Revenue ~$3.8M between July 2024 and July 2025 — implies the 2026 round prices well ahead of revenue but matches the consumer-velocity story.

## Product
- System-wide dictation for macOS and Windows (Linux on roadmap).
- Hotkey to dictate anywhere — chat boxes, docs, email, terminals, IDE prompts.
- AI cleanup layer: filler-word removal, punctuation, formatting, tone adjustment.
- Cross-app context awareness — formats output differently depending on the target app (Slack vs Gmail vs Cursor).
- Closed API in pilot with select enterprises and hardware partners; broader developer access expected later in 2026.

## Pricing
- Free tier with monthly word limit.
- Pro: ~$15/month.
- Teams and Enterprise tiers with admin controls and ZDR options.

## Differentiators
- Distribution velocity — the only voice tool in this cluster signing enterprises at the pace usually associated with horizontal SaaS, not accessibility tooling.
- Cross-app polish — Flow is the dictation app that "just works" in non-developer apps too, which is why the Fortune 500 footprint extends beyond engineering teams.
- The Wispr-hardware roadmap (neural wristband) gives them a credible long-game beyond microphones, even if Flow is the only thing earning revenue today.
- Less code-specific than Aqua — Wispr is broader productivity, not coding-first, but its developer footprint is large enough that it shows up alongside Aqua in Claude Code power-user setups.
