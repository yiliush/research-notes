# Apiiro

Apiiro is the ASPM (Application Security Posture Management) pioneer — a category positioning that emphasizes risk visibility and prioritization across the whole code-to-cloud lifecycle rather than discrete scan results. The company raised $100M Series B in late 2022 (one of the largest application security rounds at the time), and operates as a less hype-driven, enterprise-grade competitor to [[Snyk]] and [[Endor Labs]].

## Origin

Founded in 2018 in New York by Idan Plotnik and Yonatan Eldar, both Israeli security veterans. Initially focused on cloud-native application security with deep code-graph analysis.

## Funding & Traction

- Total raised: ~$135M across two rounds.
- Series B (Nov 2022): $100M led by General Catalyst with Greylock and Kleiner Perkins.
- No major round disclosed since 2022.
- Customers include F500 banks, healthcare, retail enterprises.
- Palo Alto Networks acquisition talks reportedly fell through in 2022 just before the Series B.

## Product

Apiiro's ASPM platform:

1. **Code-to-cloud risk graph.** Maps relationships between code, services, secrets, infrastructure, and runtime context.
2. **Change-driven analysis.** Detects when a code change introduces a new attack surface or risky behavior.
3. **Dev-to-prod context.** Combines SCM, CI/CD, runtime, identity, and cloud signals to prioritize risk.
4. **Compliance and policy automation.** SOX, PCI, HIPAA workflows.

## Pricing

Enterprise; not publicly disclosed. Six- to seven-figure annual ACVs typical.

## Differentiators

- **ASPM category framing.** Apiiro essentially defined the term and remains the canonical ASPM vendor.
- **Code-graph depth.** Deeper architectural analysis than scan-only competitors.
- **Enterprise security pedigree.** Strong fit for regulated industries.

## Risk factors

- No new funding signal in ~3 years.
- [[Snyk]] and [[Endor Labs]] are both moving into ASPM territory.
- Cloud security incumbents (Wiz, Palo Alto Cortex) have lateral incentives to build ASPM features.
